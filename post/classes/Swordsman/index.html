January 01, 0001
Classes

Swordsman

Still the most dominant style of fighting, Swordsmen continue the long history and lineage of the best warriors on the continent. Fighting alongside the power of the scales, swordsmen triumph with swift and decisive slashes, pushing the essence in their bodies to the limit, and creating art with their movements.

Tradition

The most practiced and distinguished sword techniques hail from the great sword sects across the continent. These techniques employ basic sword maneuvers imbued with the power of the scales. Masters of these sects can change the course of history with one attack. The three main sword sects battle for power between themselves. The title of the greatest sword sect is often debated within the public and fought for in wars. Swordsmen of these sects are deeply loyal, but don’t be misconstrued, a swordsman’s greatest loyalty exists for their sword, and if a sect falls, very few will have the resolve to continue its legacy.

One with the sword

The greatest achievement a swordsman can strive for is sword-body unification. To the swordsman, the sword is the source of their power, and an instrument to channel the power of the scales into their techniques. The greatest swordsmen on the continent are indistinguishable from the sword itself. Before the thought can even manifest in their minds, the sword is already in motion. In legends, it is said that the pinnacle of swordsmanship techniques no longer requires a sword at all, with the practitioner acting as an instrument for the scales essence themselves.

Quick Build

You can make a Swordsman quickly by following these suggestions. First, Strength should be your highest ability score, followed by Intelligence or Constitution. Second, choose the city watch or soldier background.

Class Features

As a Swordsman, you gain the following class features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d10 per Swordsman level
  • Hit Points at 1st Level: 10 + your Constitution modifier
  • Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d10 (or 6) + your Constitution modifier per Swordsman level after 1st

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light armor, Medium Armor
  • Weapons: Sword Weapons(Great-Sword., Long-Sword, Rapier)
  • Tools: None
  • Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity
  • Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Intimidation, Persuasion, Insight

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) Chain shirt or (b) Leather Armor
  • (a) a Diplomats Pack or (b) an Explorer’s pack
  • (a) a Great-sword or (b) a Rapier
  • (a) a Long-sword

Selective foundation

Starting at 1st level, and again at 6th level, you adopt a particular style of fighting as your specialty. Choose one of the following options. You can’t take a Fighting Style option more than once, even if you later get to choose again.

Defense

While you are wearing armor, you gain a +1 bonus to AC.

Dueling

When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a +2 bonus to damage rolls with that weapon.

Great Weapon Fighting

When you roll a 1 or 2 on a damage die for an attack you make with a melee weapon that you are wielding with two hands, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll, even if the new roll is a 1 or a 2. The weapon must have the two-handed or versatile property for you to gain this benefit.

Protection

When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you that is within 5 feet of you, you can use your reaction to impose disadvantage on the attack roll. You must be wielding a shield.

Swordsmanship school

The path of every swordsman begins as a student. Starting at 2nd level, choose a school and corresponding scale in which your power stems from detailed at the end of the class Features. You may use your action to cast a cantrip of the scale of your chosen school. You gain additional features at 5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th levels.

Protector of Justice

Starting at 3rd level, due to your unyielding willpower, you may push yourself beyond your normal limits for a moment. On your turn, you can take one additional action. Once you use this feature, you must finish a short or long rest before you can use it again. Starting at 13th level, you can use it twice before a rest, but only once on the same turn.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Essence

Starting at 5th level, you have learned how to capture and spend essence on sword techniques of your school. The amount of essence your body can withstand is listed on the class table.

Multidisciplinary

Starting at 7th level, your body has become tempered enough to withstand multiple essence types inside your body. You may choose an additional scale to act as a secondary essence source. You cannot choose the same scale as your Swordsmanship school. You may cast cantrips of the chosen scale.

Wood- You gain proficiency in the (a) Nature skill or (b) Animal Handling skill
Fire- You gain proficiency in the (a) Persuasion skill or (b) deception skill
Earth- You gain proficiency in the (a) Medicine skill or (b) History skill
Metal- You gain proficiency in the (a) Survival skill or (b) Athletics skill
Water- You gain proficiency in the (a) Insight skill or (b) Investigation skill

Sword Aura

Starting at the 10th level, you have gained the ability to imbue your sword with both essence types in your body. When using a Technique, your sword is imbued with the essence of your secondary essence (Your choice for Multidisciplinary). This essence adds an additional effect to you attack based on your choice.

Wood- Your techniques inflict poison until the end of their next turn.
Fire- Your techniques do an additional 1d6 in fire damage.
Earth- Your techniques Stun Enemies until the start of their turn.
Metal- Your techniques do an additional 1d6 in piercing damage
Water- Your techniques do an additional 1d6 in acid damage

Extra Attack

Beginning at 11th level, you can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn. You may only use one technique a turn.

Sword Body

Starting at the 14th level, you have become one with your sword. You know all of its strengths and flaws and can fight together as one. You may apply Sword Aura to all attacks.

Essence Recovery

At 15th level, you attain the pinnacle of resilience in battle. At the start of each of your turns, you regain hit points equal to 3 + your Constitution modifier if you have no more than half of your hit points left. You don’t gain this benefit if you have 0 hit points.

Sword Will

At 18th level, you may attack with all proficient weapons at a range of 15. All techniques have range increased by 15

Essence Avatar

At 20th level, you gain the ability to Transform into a being made entirely of Essence. As an action, you can magically become an avatar of the scales, gaining the following benefits for 1 minute: You have resistance to all damage. Techniques cost 0 essence. You may use multiple unique techniques during a turn. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Swordsmanship Schools

Different swordsman exercise different fighting styles and techniques based on the school they follow. A swordsman owes their life to the school and protects it at all costs.

Starting at 2nd level, you gain the ability to cast cantrips of the chosen scale as an action. The school you choose dictates what scale you imbue into yourself.

Starting at 5th level, and again at 9th, 13th, and 17th levels, you learn a technique of your sword school. The sword art is listed in the school’s description in order. You learn the first technique in the set at 5th level, 2nd in the set at 9th level, and so on. You may spend an amount of essence to use a technique of your sword school as an action if you are using a proficient weapon.

Your total essence is listed in the class table. Each ability you use costs points corresponding to its order. For example, a schools 1st technique costs 1 essence while a schools 4th technique costs 4. Spent essence are refreshed after a short or long rest.

Abilities requiring concentration become interrupted if hit by an attack or making an attack.

Your DC for cantrips and techniques = 8 + your proficiency bonus + Your Inteligence modifier

Mount Hua

The Mount Hua Sect is located on top of a mountain near the wood scale. Techniques from this school utilize control and synthesis with nature. Mastery of this technique can cause plum blossoms to form as a byproduct of the essence of the wood scale being expelled. Often the smell of plum blossoms linger on battlefield after a master has fought. While some may scoff at the beautiful nature of these techniques, masters of this technique, nicknamed blossom swordsman, are some of the most sharp and meticulous on the continent.

One with the flowers

Starting at 1st level, you imbue yourself with the essence of the Wood scale. Gain proficiency in the Nature and Animal Handling Skills.

Mount Hua Techniques

Plum blossom Sword 1st technique: slash

  • Essence cost: 1
  • Damage: 3d6 slashing damage
  • Effects: The slash of your sword imitates the strength of roots cutting through stone. You make a melee attack against a target within your reach. On a hit, you deal 3d6 slashing damage and regain hit points equal to half the amount of damage dealt.

Plum blossom Sword 2nd technique: Petal Blast

  • Essence cost: 2
  • Damage: 6d8 piercing damage
  • Effects: A wave of peach blossom petals shoot out from the tip of your blade into a 60ft cone. You make a ranged attack against every creature inside this area. They take 6d8 piercing damage on hit.

Plum blossom Sword 3rd technique: bloom

  • Essence cost: 3
  • Damage: 5d10 slashing damage
  • Effects: The smell of peach blossoms radiate out within a 30 ft area of yourself for 10 minutes or until you lose concentration. All allies that end their turn in this area gain 3d8 health points. You may make a melee attack against any enemies that enter that radius, dealing 5d10 slashing damage on hit.

Plum blossom Sword 4th technique: Protect

  • Essence cost: 4
  • Damage: N/A
  • Effects: Until your concentration ends, or after 5 minutes, you are immune to all damage.

Mystical Sword

The Mystical Sword Sect is a largely unknown sect located in the northern taiga, just outside the Space Scale. The capital warriors of this sect are trained specifically to protect the space scale, but the sect welcomes all who have talent in fighting with the scales. Warriors of this sect are some of the most feared in the continent, few and far between, their sword skills are largely unknown.

Student of the space between

Starting at 1st level, you gain Mastery of the Space scale, Gain proficiency in the Arcana Skill.

Mystical Sword Techniques

Space Principle 1: Cut

  • Essence cost: 1
  • Damage: 2d8 slashing damage
  • Effects: You slash with the precision to cut through reality itself. You may melee attack up to three targets within 10 ft of you, dealing 2d8 slashing damage on hit.

Space Principle 2: Blink

  • Essence cost: 2
  • Damage: 5d10 force damage
  • Effects: You point your weapon at a target within 30 ft, directing the space around you. You teleport within 5 ft of them and make a melee attack. On hit, they take 5d10 force damage.

Space Principle 3: Void

  • Essence cost: 3
  • Damage: 8d8 psychic damage
  • Effects: Choose a point within 100 ft of yourself. At that point, void spreads within a 50 ft radius sphere for 10 minutes or until you lose concentration. No creature can see through the darkness. Whenever a creature starts its turn in the sphere, you make a melee attack against it. On hit, you deal 6d8 psychic damage.

Space Principle 4: Exile

  • Essence cost: 4
  • Damage: N/A
  • Effects: You banish a creature into the space between worlds. Choose a creature with your reach and make a melee attack against it. On hit, the creature must make a charisma saving throw with DC equal to 8 + your Strength modifier. If the creature fails the save, it is transported into the void.

Pure Fire

The Pure Fire Sect is located in the mountains of Agawrathe, fueled by the essence of the fire scale. The sect holds a major position of power in the Agawrathe region, with access to a large amount of minerals but also the combat abilities to protect them. The warriors of the sect are rugged, fearless, and strong. Weather or not they are respected by the other sects of the continent, the martial community cannot deny the Pure fire sect to be one of the powers of the continent.

Watcher of the many embers

Starting at 1st level, you gain Mastery of the Fire scale. Gain proficiency in the Persuasion and Deception Skills.

Pure Fire Techniques

Origin flame first movement: pierce

  • Essence cost: 1
  • Damage: 3d8 fire Damage
  • Effects: You lunge out with your weapon creating sparks in the air. You make a melee attack against a creature within your reach. On hit, they take 3d8 fire damage.

Origin flame second movement: Fire Wall

  • Essence cost: 2
  • Damage: 4d8 fire damage
  • Effects: A wall of fire extends from the slash of your sword. You make a melee attack against any targets in a line from yourself to 60 ft away, dealing 4d8 fire damage on hit. The wall persists for 10 minutes, or until you loose concentration. The wall blocks line of sight, and deals 2d8 fire damage to any creature who passes through it.

Origin flame third movement: Birth of swords

  • Essence cost: 3
  • Damage: 4d12 fire damage
  • Effects: Seven swords of flame orbit around your body. Until you lose concentration, or until all the swords are expended, you may use a bonus action to send a sword streaking at a target within 120 ft of you. You make a ranged spell attack. On hit, you deal 4d12 fire damage.

Origin flame fourth movement: Kill

  • Essence cost: 4
  • Damage: N/A
  • Effects: Make an inconspicuous melee attack against a creature within range. On hit, If the creature has 100 hit points of fewer, it dies. On miss, the creature dies if it has 50 hit points or fewer.
