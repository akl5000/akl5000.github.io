Beggar

From the naive eye, beggars look like a plague that coat the cities across the continent. But others, those who understand the wisdom hidden behind the beggars few words, understand the true worth of those people.

Wisdom from the Land

Beggars are the brain of the continent. A band of brothers making up the poorest people of the continent, but with the skills to rival the major guilds. Alone, a beggar can become a formidable foe, but together, their combined wisdom and experiences can make armies crumble. The most powerful of these people, with the worldly experience second to none, can harness the essence of the scales to conjure up magic beyond imagination.

Information Network

The information network of major guild can’t match the massive web the beggar’s guild has laid across the continent. Beggars, quite opposite to their name, provide information and services found nowhere else. Maps, information, strategies, and general wisdom, all are common trades. Only a possession-less, stoic, lifestyle can maintain the burden of the scale’s essence, and the beggars have perfected this skill. With the ability to master more essences than any others, nobody is more suited for spell casting than a determined beggar.

Quick Build

You can make a Beggar quickly by following these suggestions. First, Intelligence should be your highest ability score, followed by Constitution or Dexterity. Second, choose the sage background.

Class Features

As a Beggar, you gain the following class features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d6 per Beggar level
  • Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
  • Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per Beggar level after 1st

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light armor
  • Weapons: Simple Weapons
  • Tools: Herbalism kit and Navigator’s tools
  • Saving Throws: Intelligence and Wisdom
  • Skills: Choose three from Sleight of Hand, Stealth, Insight, Investigation, Survival, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Animal Handling

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) Hide or (b) Leather Armor
  • (a) a Forgery kit or (b) a Disguise kit
  • (a) a hand axe or (b) a dagger
  • an Explorer’s Pack and an Herbalism kit
  • a cane (Quarterstaff)

Spellcasting

Casting a spell requires the equivalent cost of the scale’s essence. The 5 core scales (wood, fire, earth, metal, water) have a variable assigned to them based on the essence contained in the player. This value ranges from 1-10 and acts as the resource cost of a spell. The value of this cost is outlined in the class table under essence limit. After a short rest, points may be distributed into the 5 values in any order equal to (Essence/2 * Scales) rounded up. After a long rest, all values are set to max.

Control and Rage essence values work differently. The value for each of these range between 0-10 and mirrors each other. If rage is spent as a spells resource cost, the amount spent is added to the control value and vice versa. If all rage is spent on spells, additional rage can only be gained by spending control essence on spells. After a long rest, both the control and rage values are set to 5.

Spells requiring Darkness as a resource cost can only be cast in darkness or shadow. You have unlimited dark essence, but your essence limit still dictates what dark spells you can cast.

Spells requiring Space as a resource cost can be cast similarly to the 5 core scales, but requires essence taken from all 5 core scales. For example, a spell with a dark essence cost 2 would take 2 essences from all the core essences, even if they are not unlocked.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Essence Capture

Your experience among the streets has given you great understanding over the essence of the scales. Choose a scale from the list below, whatever scale you choose, you gain the skill proficiency it offers and the ability to cast spells and cantrips with the scale’s essence. You are offered an additional choice from this list at the 3rd, 6th, 10th, 14th, and 18th level.

Wood- You gain proficiency in the (a) Nature skill or (b) Animal Handling skill
Fire- You gain proficiency in the (a) Persuasion skill or (b) Deception skill
Earth- You gain proficiency in the (a) Medicine skill or (b) History skill
Metal- You gain proficiency in the (a) Survival skill or (b) Athletics skill
Water- You gain proficiency in the (a) Insight skill or (b) Investigation skill
Control/Rage- You gain proficiency in the (a) Perception skill or (b) Intimidation skill
Dark- You gain proficiency in the Stealth skill
Space- You gain proficiency in the Arcana skill

Situational Awareness

Starting at 2nd level, you can add your Intelligence modifiers to your initiative rolls.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Essence Deflection

At 5th level, you have learned to weave your essence to fortify yourself against harm. When you are hit by an attack or you fail a saving throw, you can use your reaction to gain a +2 bonus to your AC against that attack or a +4 bonus to that saving throw. When you use this feature, you can’t cast spells other than cantrips until the end of your next turn.

Essence overload

Starting at 9th level, as a bonus action, you may reduce your total essence limit of all scales by 1 to gain essence equal to your new essence max that you may distribute freely. Your essence limit increases by 1 after a short rest and increases to its maximum after a long rest.

Trance of the Stoic

Starting at 13th level, you gain the ability to align your consciousness to the endless flow of essence. As a bonus action, you can enter this state for 1 minute. For the duration, attack rolls against you can’t benefit from advantage, and whenever you make an attack roll, an ability check, or a saving throw, you can treat a roll of 9 or lower on the d20 as a 10. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Unearthly Recovery

At 17th level, you gain the ability to overcome grievous injuries. As a bonus action when you have fewer than half of your hit points remaining, you can regain a number of hit points equal to half your hit point maximum. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Body of essence

At 20th level, your body is indistinguishable from the essence all around us. You maintain a physical form, but insightful people see pure magical energy instead. You may cast spells 3 cost or less without expending essence.
