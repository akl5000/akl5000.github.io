Ranger

Far from the bustle of cities and towns, past the most distant farms close to the terrors of the wild, amid the dense-packed trees of trackless forests and across wide and empty plains, rangers keep their unending watch. While the bow and arrow are considered lowly compared to the fierce close quartered arts of the sword or spear, a true master of the bow can make any person, no matter the strength, feel real despair. Rangers choose the bow as their weapon not because they want the easy path forward, but because they know the continent will not be safe without them.

Eyes of a predator.

Bow arts have existed since the dawn of civilization. Before steel, a bow existed as the only layer of protection against the powers of the wilderness. Pure, primal, strength and dexterity fuel the ranger’s fights. Unlike the rest of the distinguished fighting styles across the continent, the bow arts did not draw its power from the scales, but the untouched eye of a physical being. An eye that sees everything as prey.

Embellished by essence

The discovery of essence posed a threat to the ranger’s livelihood, as more forms of combat entered play. But the bow arts did not miss a beat, developing a unique style that uses essence not as a source of power, but an article of precision in a keen rangers’ hands. Arrows made of essence not only changed the life of rangers, but made their participation in battles across the world necessary. As much as the world needed them. The bow arts were not for the faint of heart, and could only be learned under the most primal of conditions. This environment made the return of the ranger from their training in the wild a celebratory time for their home, as their kingdom had just gained another hero.

Quick Build

You can make a Ranger quickly by following these suggestions. First, Dexterity should be your highest ability score, followed by Intelligence. Second, choose the outlander background.

Class Features

As a Ranger, you gain the following class features

Hit Points

  • Hit Dice: 1d8 per Bowman level
  • Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
  • Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per Ranger level after 1st

Proficiencies

  • Armor: Light armor, Medium Armor
  • Weapons: Ranged Weapons
  • Tools: Herbalism kit
  • Saving Throws: Strength, Intelligence
  • Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Arcana, Investigation, Insight, Perception, Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) Hide or (b) Leather Armor
  • (a) a Dagger or*(b)* a Hand-Axe
  • (a) a Longbow or (b) Light Crossbow
  • a short bow, Explorer’s pack, and Quiver with 30 arrows

Archery

You gain a +2 bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons and your ranged weapon attacks ignore three-quarters cover.

Specialized training

You have the choice between specializing in long- or short-range attacks. Choose one of the specialties listed below. The specialty will grant you features again at 6th level.

Close Combative Archery

Beginning at 2nd level, being within 5 feet of a hostile creature doesn’t impose disadvantage on your ranged attack rolls. If you are within 10 ft of an enemy. At 6th level, you ignore the loading quality of crossbows with which you are proficient.

Deadeye

Beginning at 2nd level, attacking at long range doesn’t impose disadvantage on your ranged weapon attack rolls.

At 6th level, when you hit a target under the normal range of a weapon, the creature suffers normal damage and must make a Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.

Eye for power

Even the ancient art of archery cannot escape the scale’s influence. Starting at the 3rd level, choose a scale in which to base your archery. Your choice will grant additional features at 8th and 14th level.

Metal Scale
Starting at 3rd level, you gain proficiency in the (a) Survival skill or (b) Athletics skill. Your arrow tips are imbued with essence. Your ranged attacks deal an additional 1d4 piercing damage.

Starting at 8th level, your attacks pierce your target. If your attack lands, you may make an attack roll with disadvantage for every target in a line directly behind your target.

At 14th level, your ranged weapon attacks score a critical hit on a roll of 19-20, and you regain a focus point on a roll of 19 or 20 on a d20 attack roll.

Water Scale
Starting at 3rd level, you gain proficiency in the (a) Insight skill or (b) Investigation skill. Your arrow tips are imbued with essence. Your ranged attacks deal an additional 1d4 piercing damage.

Starting at 8th level, your ranged weapon attacks ignore half full cover.

At 14th level, you learn how to direct an errant arrow toward a new target. When you make an attack roll with a ranged weapon and miss, you can use a bonus action to reroll the attack roll against a different target within 60 feet of the original target.

Ability Score Improvement

When you reach 4th level, and again at 7th, 9th, 12th, 15th, 17th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature. ###Essence tips Starting at 7th level, you have learned to imbue the power of essence into your arrows. Your ranged attacks an additional 2d6 piercing damage on hit.

Focus

By 5th level, you have mastered your breathing and have been able to make more and more impressive shots. Beginning at level five you may choose to spend focus points to attempt a calculated shot. Focus refreshes to max after a short or long rest. Types of shots are listed at the bottom of class features. Some of your calculated shots require your targets to make a saving throw to resist the shot’s effects. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:
calculated shot save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier

Shapeless Archery

Starting at the 9th level, you have employed the scales essence to form arrows, you no longer require ammunition to fire ranged weapons. Your ranged attacks do an additional piercing damage. You may make the attack magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage (the damage type changes to force).

Expertise

At 11th level, choose two of your skill proficiencies, Your proficiency bonus is doubled for any ability check you make that uses either of the chosen proficiencies.

MultiShot

Starting at 13th level, before you make an attack with a ranged weapon, you may choose two targets and fire at both. For each additional arrow fired, remove one arrow die from that arrows damage.

Daunting confidence

Starting at 16th level, before you make an attack with a ranged weapon that you are proficient with, you can choose to take a -1 penalty to the attack roll. If that attack hits, you add 1d6 piercing to the attack’s damage. You may do this any number of times per attack.

Triple Shot

Starting at 18th level, before you make an attack with a ranged weapon, you may choose three targets and fire at all of them with the same attack type. For each additional arrow fired, remove one arrow die from that arrows damage.

Quad Shot

Starting at 20th level, before you make an attack with a ranged weapon, you may choose four targets and fire at all of them with the same attack type. For each additional arrow fired, remove one arrow die from that arrows damage.

Calculated Shots

Intimidation Shot

You can use the thundering sound of your bow to shake the resolve of a creature. You can expend one focus point while making a Charisma (Intimidation) check to gain advantage on the roll.

Dazing Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature, you can expend one focus point to attempt to dizzy your opponent. On a hit, the creature suffers normal damage and must make a Constitution saving throw or suffer disadvantage on attacks until the end of their next turn.

Steady Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature who cannot see you, you can expend one focus point to gain advantage on the attack roll.

Disarming Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature, you can expend one focus point to attempt to shoot an object from their hands. On a hit, the creature suffers normal damage and must succeed on a Strength saving throw or drop 1 held object of your choice and have that object be pushed 10 feet away from them.

Forceful Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature, you can expend one focus point to attempt to trip them up and force them back. On a hit, the creature suffers normal damage and must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed 15 feet away from you.

Power Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature, you can expend one or more focus points to enhance the volatility of the attack. For each focus point expended, the attack gains a -1 to the attack role. If the attack hits, you can roll one additional weapon damage die per focus point spent when determining the damage.

Explosive Shot

When you make a ranged attack against a creature, you can expend one focus point to attempt to topple a moving target. On a hit, the creature suffers normal damage and must make a Strength saving throw or be knocked prone.
